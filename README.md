# Exploring the Relationship Between National Happiness and Higher Education Prestige

## Project Overview
This project aims to investigate the potential relationship between national happiness levels and the prestige of a country's higher education system. By combining data from the **World Happiness Report** and **Global University Rankings**, we will analyze whether nations with more top-ranked universities exhibit higher happiness scores.

## Motivation
A strong higher education system is often associated with positive societal outcomes, including innovation, economic growth, and improved quality of life. The central hypothesis of this project is that countries with a robust higher education infrastructure—evidenced by a higher concentration of top-ranked universities—may also demonstrate higher levels of national happiness. Insights derived from this analysis could help inform educational policy and investment strategies.

## Data Sources

### 1. World Happiness Report Data (`world_happiness_data_2024.csv`)
- **Description:**  
  This dataset contains national happiness scores for various countries, including key indicators such as GDP per capita, social support, healthy life expectancy, freedom to make life choices, generosity, and perceptions of corruption.
- **Source and Access:**  
  - Provided as a CSV file (`world_happiness_data_2024.csv`).
  - **Kaggle Reference:** [World Happiness Report on Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/unsdsn/world-happiness) 

### 2. Global University Rankings Data (`global_ranking_universities_2024.csv`)
- **Description:**  
  This dataset includes information on the global rankings of universities, detailing ranking positions of top institutions by country. This data can be aggregated to assess the overall strength of a country’s higher education system.
- **Source and Access:**  
  - Provided as a CSV file (`global_ranking_universities_2024.csv`).
  - **Kaggle Reference:** [Global University Rankings on Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/gauravduttakiit/world-university-rankings) 

## Project Proposal and Methodology

### Objectives
- **Primary Objective:**  
  Determine if there is a correlation between national happiness scores and the quality of higher education as measured by global university rankings.
- **Secondary Objective:**  
  Identify patterns or trends that might inform policy decisions regarding education and national well-being.
